COMMON QUALITATIVE TESTS IN ORGANIC CHEMISTRY

in sci, sci.chem

Rarer and rarer do the qualitative chemical tests become the primary tool in the identification of organic compounds, but the understanding of their underlying principles remains crucial to build up confidence and competence in the chemical realm. The standard A-level curriculum in the UK requires the knowledge of the reactions with the following reagents:

  • boiling Fehling's or Benedict's solution

    Both reagents are suitable to differentiate between almost all aldehydes and ketones.

    Fehling's reagent is a solution of copper (II) ions complexed with tartrate ions in presence of sodium hydroxide:

  • acidified potassium dichromate (VI) solution

  • phosphorous (V) chloride

  • sodium/potassium carbonate or hydrogencarbonate solution

  • 2,4-dinitrophenylhydrazine solution

  • bromine water

  • warm aqueous sodium/potassium hydroxide, dilute nitric acid and aqueous silver nitrate

  • warm ammoniacal silver nitrate solution, called Tollen's reagent

  • small piece of sodium

  • warm carboxylic acid with a few drops of concentrated sulphuric acid

  • sodium nitrite and dilute hydrochloric acid followed by an alkaline solution of phenol in ice-cold condition

  • iodine in alkaline solution

  • lithium tetrahydridoaluminate in dry ether
