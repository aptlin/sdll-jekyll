The list is a compilation of resources on logic, as recommended by C.B.Allendoerfer and C.O.Oakley in their book "Principles of Mathematics", with the links to the digital works available in the Net.

1.  "The Logic of Modern Physics" by P.W. Bridgman, pp. 1-32

      The electronic copy of the 1958 edition can be found there.

2.  "Logical Nonsense" by Lewis Carrol, pp. 505-546

3.  "What Is Mathematics?" by R.Courant and H.Robbins, p. 22

4.  "Fate and Freedom" by J.Frank, pp. 458-469

5.  "The Elements of Mathematical Logic" by P.Rosenbloom, pp. 28-68

6.  "An Introduction to Mathematical Thought" by E.R.Stabler, pp. 1-119

7.  "Introduction to Logic" by Alfred Tarski, pp.3-140

      The book is digitised and can be accessed there.

8.  "Philosophy of Mathematics and Natural Science" by H.Weyl, pp. 3-29

The following articles in the American Mathematical Monthly can be referenced also:

  1. "Geometry and Empirical Science" by C.G.Hempel
  2. "On the Nature of Mathematical Truth" by C.G.Hempel
  3. "Symbolic Logic" by Saunders MacLane
  4. "The Nature of Mathematical Proof" by R.L.Wilder
